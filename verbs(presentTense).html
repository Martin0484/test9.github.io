Verbs: Present Tense

Conjugations are the most important thing to know about spanish verbs. The conjugation of the verb depends on what pronoun is used with the verb and the type of the verb. Here's a list of spanish pronouns:

yo (I), tu (you), el (he), ella (she), usted(you), nosotros (we), ustedes (you all)


And here are the three types of verbs:

AR verbs: verbs that end in -ar

Examples: caminar (to walk), estar (to be), tomar (to drink), comprar (to buy)

ER verbs: verbs that end in -er

Examples: comer (to eat), aprender (to learn), tener (to have), correr (to run)

IR verbs: verbs that end in -ir

Examples: abrir (to open), vivir (to live), escribir (to know), dormir (to sleep)


To conjugate each type of verb, you need to get rid of the last two letters and add an ending. Here's the ending for each type of verb and each pronoun:

Pronoun    AR Verb Ending    ER Verb Ending    IR Verb Ending
   yo    -o    -o    -o
   tu    -as    -es    -es
   el/ella/usted    -a    -e    -e
   nosotros    -amos    -emos    -imos
   ustedes/ellos/ellas    -an    -en    -en
